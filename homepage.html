Episode 9: Mid-Majors Go Dancin'

Did your favorite mid-major team go dancing? Jordan Klein takes a look at some of the best mid-major teams this year with our analysts Jake Riepma and Darren Zaslau. Check it out to find out more about their Cinderella team, the Loyola Ramblers and a new rapid fire round.

Jordan Klein

Jordan Klein, 23, graduated cum laude from Vanderbilt University in 2017, majoring in Communication Studies with a minor in Corporate Strategy.

During his time at Vanderbilt, he was a recipient of the Frank K. Houston Public Speaking Award, Vice President of Vanderbilt Tour Guides and his viral high school commencement address was featured in publications such as the Chicago Tribune.

In November of 2017, he returned to Vanderbilt to deliver his first TED talk about the value of an apprenticeship for college students.

Jordan is now pursuing his Master of Science in Journalism at Northwestern University, specializing in Sports Media, Documentary Filmmaking and Broadcast Video. He also serves as a research assistant for multiple NYT bestselling author Jonathan Eig.

After graduating from Northwestern in August 2018, Jordan's goal is to share the most inspiring human-interest stories in the world of sports and beyond.

Episode 9: Champions League

In honor of the first and second week of the Champions League Round of 16 second leg, Brianna Williams talks with Emmett McConnell and Erik Alcantar about their reactions to PSG and Real Madrid and preview Man United v. Sevilla and Chelsea v. Barcelona.

X

Brianna Williams

Brianna, 24, is an aspiring sports reporter in the sport of baseball.

Prior to Medill, she received a degree in Sport Management and Business Administration from the University of Tampa.

She is most knowledgeable in baseball, football, volleyball and basketball, but has a strong interest in learning any sport.

Emmett McConnell

Emmett played goalkeeper at Wesleyan University, graduating in 2015 with a bachelor's degree in Economics.

He then worked at a beat writer for Prost Amerika, covering the Philadelphia Union over the course of the 2016 and 2017 seasons.

If you ask him about soccer, beware what you get yourself into, as he will talk your ear off.

Erik Alcantar

Erik is a Chicago born and Indiana raised MSJ student who loves sports and can't get enough of them. Hopeless White Sox fan, early 2000s Lakers bandwagoner, Colts fan from Bears territory, and American soccer believer with teams from across the pond (Chelsea FC and Atletico Madrid).

Dreaming to be a sports commentator, Erik's inspirations include Ken "Hawk" Harrelson, Ray Hudson, Al Michaels, Mike Breen and others.

Prior to Northwestern, Erik received a degree in Telecommunications from Ball State University. He also concentrated in Emerging Media and received a minor in Spanish

While at BSU, he did women's soccer play-by-play and color commentary for the student radio station, WCRD. He also co-hosted a weekly fantasy football show called The Fantasy Express.
