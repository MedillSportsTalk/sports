Erik Alcantar

Erik is a Chicago born and Indiana raised MSJ student who loves sports and can't get enough of them. Hopeless White Sox fan, early 2000s Lakers bandwagoner, Colts fan from Bears territory, and American soccer believer with teams from across the pond (Chelsea FC and Atletico Madrid).

Dreaming to be a sports commentator, Erik's inspirations include Ken "Hawk" Harrelson, Ray Hudson, Al Michaels, Mike Breen and others.

Patrick Engel

Prior to Northwestern, Patrick, 23, graduated from Southern Methodist University in May 2017 with a Bachelor's degree in journalism.

Currently, he is a reporter and the managing editor for PonyStampede.com, 247Sports.com’s SMU site, where he covers SMU men's basketball and recruiting.

During his time in Dallas, his responsibilities also included football team and recruiting coverage. He has broken news of coaching hires, recruiting commitments and player transfers. Since becoming the managing editor, he has assisted in publishing stories, scheduling stories and delegating assignments.

While at SMU, Patrick also served as the associate sports editor of the school's student newspaper, The SMU Campus Weekly, and covered SMU football and basketball. Before becoming the associate sports editor, he was the sports columnist and a staff writer.

In 2016, Patrick spent a summer at FOX4News in Dallas. As a digital web intern, he worked about 25 hours per week and wrote eight to 10 stories per day on Dallas-area breaking news, crime and sports, plus other relevant national stories.

Jordan Klein

Jordan Klein, 23, graduated cum laude from Vanderbilt University in 2017, majoring in Communication Studies with a minor in Corporate Strategy.

During his time at Vanderbilt, he was a recipient of the Frank K. Houston Public Speaking Award, Vice President of Vanderbilt Tour Guides and his viral high school commencement address was featured in publications such as the Chicago Tribune.

In November of 2017, he returned to Vanderbilt to deliver his first TED talk about the value of an apprenticeship for college students.

Jordan is now pursuing his Master of Science in Journalism at Northwestern University, specializing in Sports Media, Documentary Filmmaking and Broadcast Video. He also serves as a research assistant for multiple NYT bestselling author Jonathan Eig.

After graduating from Northwestern in August 2018, Jordan's goal is to share the most inspiring human-interest stories in the world of sports and beyond.

Jake Riepma

Jake Riepma, 25, is an aspiring play-by-play broadcaster and current graduate student at the Medill School of Journalism at Northwestern University.

Before coming to Northwestern, Jake Riepma was the Voice of the Northwood University Timberwolves, providing play-by-play for men’s and women's athletic teams.

Jake earned his Bachelors of Business Administration from Northwood in December of 2015, majoring in Sports Management and Marketing.

While at Northwood, Riepma played on the football team, and following the 2015 season, he was awarded the Jack H. McAvoy Award by the Great Lakes Intercollegiate Athletic Conference (GLIAC) in recognition of outstanding character, service, and leadership throughout his tenure at Northwood.

After graduation in 2015, Riepma accepted the Assistant Sports Information Director role at Northwood where he continued his passionate work in sports media for the Timberwolves before enrolling at Northwestern in the fall of 2017.

Juliana Sherry

Juliana Sherry, 23, directs, produces, edits and stars in Medill Sports Talk.

Originally from South Bend, Ind., she participated in eight different sports from age 3 to 18.

Before Northwestern, Juliana earned her undergraduate degree from Indiana University.

She was involved in multiple extra-curricular activities, including as a play-by-play broadcaster for student radio and Big Ten Student U. Juliana also covered field hockey and water polo for the IU Athletic Department.

In the spring of her senior year, Juliana traveled with the IU softball team, broadcasting all 54 games of the 2017 season.

In her lifetime thus far, Juliana has learned how to play and broadcast 14 sports.

Jonathan Skinner

Born in Brooklyn, NY and raised in Hope Mills, NC, Jonathan Skinner is currently pursuing his Masters of Science in Journalism at Northwestern University.

Before attending Northwestern he graduated from Morehouse College with a B.A. in religion and an M.Div from Emory University.

While in his master's program at Emory he began working as a public address announcer for the football and basketball teams at his alma mater, Morehouse.

His work there fueled his desire to be part of the action. It also helped him find the exact path of sports media he wanted to pursue, sports broadcasting, play-by-play announcing and sports anchoring. All of which are skills he's learning at Northwestern University and by being part of Medill Sports Talk.
